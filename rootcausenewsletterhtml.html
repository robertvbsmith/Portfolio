Did you know being open to learning from other people's mistakes is considered wisdom?

According to the scripture, Job 12:12 KJV, “With the ancient is wisdom; and in length of days understanding.” My dad was a youth pastor at 12 years old, so he loved scripture and always sought the Truth within each one. Even though he eventually moved on from this position as he grew into his teens and did not become a pastor of his own church, my siblings and I were pretty much raised like PK’s (Pastors kids). The principles of truth and the value of seeking it in all ways, things, thoughts, and deed was his legacy to us. I grew up knowing that Truth is not only timeless, it is universal. It can be found anywhere should you decide to seek it with the desire to learn, grow, and heal. The reason why I decided to name my practice Root Cause Breathwork is because at the root of every lesson, there is a truth waiting to be discovered, embraced, and diligently applied. Root Cause Analysis in the spiritual world can seem like the oxymoron of two opposites, right? Analysis and the vastness of the unknowable. I hear you! And yet, that is pretty much the point. As in Breathwork, the further you go along in your series of ten or twenty session journey with me, the more you will hopefully discover the alignment missing in some areas of your awareness. Or as my mother (my best friend in Life and my most demonstrative Mentor) would call them, “blind spots”. We all have them. I also like to refer to these ‘blind spots’ as the spaces that exist between the extreme levels of thought, feeling, and emotion. Those subtle nuances of life, those nudges, hints, and clues that can be easily missed if not pointed out, and/or felt in the senses (taste, touch, smell, sound, and emotion) along our journey. That is why we need each other, because none of us are Omnipresent, leaving us vulnerable to bouts of unawareness about ourselves. Parts which can be discovered through the trusted, compassionate, loving relationships we put in place to give us feedback about them.

SAME MISTAKES, DIFFERENT DAY

Yes, ladies and gentlemen, before Breathwork found me that was the theme of my life. I was called hardheaded, willful, uncoachable, etc. etc. Talking more than listening was my claim to fame in my younger years. I just knew I had life all figured out, until the mistakes started piling up, and costing me money, time, and pain. Like choosing the same guy in relationships and expecting a different outcome. One of my early mentors, Byron Nelson, taught me that the definition of insanity is doing the same thing repeatedly and expecting different results. That same mentor also taught me the value in INSPECTING WHAT I EXPECT. You heard that right, INSPECT WHAT YOU EXPECT. Look over your expectations, there are clues there. Expectations are most often pitfalls in life because, no one owes us anything, we owe it all to ourselves. So, what do you expect, and from whom, and WHY? It goes deep, it really does! Most times the answers lie in forgiveness of ourselves and others. One of my Grand Teachers and current Mentors in Breathwork, Dana Darma Devi (Co-Founder of Clarity Breathwork), always says, “There is always someone or something to forgive, you never run out of either one!” It is so true! A Course in Miracles is a book that brings me back to this truth often, and a life changing read. I love the deep end, where my feet are no longer planted, and I must trust my breath to move forward. What part of the healing process do you love?

MENTORSHIP IS A SHORT-CUT

But it requires trust and an unwavering willingness to get out of your comfort zone. Don’t be afraid to have different mentors for different parts of life. We are WHOLE BEINGS. This requires a WHOLE team of support to discover our purpose, accomplish our dreams, and be healthy whole vibrant human beings! As you have probably already noticed, I have had many mentors. I will mention them in my newsletters because they all helped me avoid certain pitfalls and took years of suffering off my life just by being humble enough to share their most painful mistakes and how they overcame the pain and what wisdoms they distilled. Others (not all) held the space, held my hand, and gave me encouragement and patience when I didn’t always heed their advice. Others were not so nice nor understanding when I didn’t heed their advice or guidance, and sometimes that is a necessary part of the Journey. I learned from them as well. Some mentors are only meant to be with you for the short run, and others for the long run. Your discernment will assist you in finding which mentor(s) fit where and how. What I have learned about mentorship is that you must find harmonious mentors, ones that feel like a good fit for who you are, what you seek, where you are going, and how you want to be treated along the way. You will find that this too changes like the ebbs and flows of life. There are all kinds of mentors from all walks of life with many different approaches, so find what works for you and leave the rest. No need to judge or hold grievances. Remember, there are levels to this! Just think Neo in the Matrix, and The Oracle where she says to him in Matrix Reloaded, “We are all here to do, what we are all here to do”. Again, levels to this! I can promise you that if you choose the path of giving and receiving mentorship, the rewards can be transformative and life changing for long periods of time! My Godmother, Iris is one of my long-time mentors who shifted my entire life’s trajectory in one chance encounter that happened 30 years ago! I am most grateful that I took a chance and allowed her to teach me from her own mistakes. My dad taught me at a young age what the power of mentorship can do for your peace of mind, body, and soul when it is harmonious! These blessed earth-angels can save you time, pain, mistakes, and money. He was right! Enough about me, what is the continuous loop you seek an exit from?
